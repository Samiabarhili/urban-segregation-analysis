# Urban Economics – Urban Area Size and Socio-Spatial Segregation

> Project conducted as part of the **Urban Economics course at CY Tech (April 2025)**.

This project investigates the relationship between the **size of urban areas in France** and their **level of socio-spatial segregation**.

Using **INSEE datasets (2021)**, we analyze the social composition of urban areas and measure segregation using two indicators:

- **Duncan Index** – measures the spatial separation between executives and workers (binary measure)
- **Entropy Index** – measures the overall diversity of social categories

---

## Key Findings

- Larger urban areas tend to have a **higher proportion of executives** and a **lower proportion of workers**.
- **Segregation slightly increases with urban size**  
  - R² ≈ 4% for the Duncan index  
  - R² ≈ 27% for the entropy index
- Large cities are generally **more socially diverse**, but **not necessarily more spatially mixed**.
